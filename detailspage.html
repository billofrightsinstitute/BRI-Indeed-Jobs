Job Description:

Are you passionate about transforming education and building partnerships that empower educators and students? Join our team at the Bill of Rights Institute as Manager, Program Partnerships. In this role, you will play a pivotal role in establishing and managing relationships with school districts and educators, driving sales opportunities, and fostering collaborations to advance civic education initiatives. If you excel in dynamic environments and are committed to making a tangible impact in education, this position is perfect for you.

Responsibilities:

  • Establish and maintain relationships with school districts and educators to facilitate collaboration and support the adoption of resources from the Bill of Rights Institute.
  • Collaborate with key stakeholders to identify and cultivate leads for program opportunities.
  • Initiate and oversee partnerships with state social studies councils, historical societies, museums, and other civic education organizations to broaden outreach and impact.
  • Represent the Bill of Rights Institute at social studies conferences by staffing vendor tables, promoting awareness, and fostering connections that may lead to partnerships and programs.
  • Conduct market research and outreach within designated regions to compile lists of potential partnership prospects.
  • Monitor and manage program budgets, collaborating with our Development team to ensure accurate reporting and compliance with financial requirements.
  • Provide leadership in delivering professional development sessions for classroom teachers, emphasizing the effective use of resources from the Bill of Rights Institute, and incorporating best pedagogical practices.
  • Work with our Manager, Program Implementation and Programs Coordinator to design and implement programs nationwide.
  • Oversee the management of school district relationships from initial engagement through program completion, ensuring satisfaction and successful outcomes.
  • Allocate 30% of your schedule to travel, engaging with stakeholders, attending conferences, and conducting outreach activities, as necessary.

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in education or related field (Master's degree preferred).
  • Proven experience in cultivating and managing relationships with school districts, educators, and educational organizations.
  • Strong business development skills, with a track record of identifying and pursuing educational partnership opportunities.
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills, with the ability to effectively engage diverse stakeholders.
  • Knowledge of educational trends, curriculum standards, and pedagogical strategies in social studies education preferred.
  • Exceptional organizational and project management abilities, capable of juggling multiple priorities and deadlines effectively.
  • A willingness to travel frequently and work flexible hours as needed to fulfill the demands of the role.

If you are enthusiastic about driving positive change in education and are eager to play a pivotal role in advancing civic education by supporting educators, we encourage you to apply for this exciting opportunity. Join us in empowering educators and students with the resources they need to become informed and engaged citizens.

Your cover letter and resume should demonstrate your writing skills and accuracy as well as let us know why you want to work at the Bill of Rights Institute.

Please apply directly through our website at

https://billofrightsinstitute.org/about-bri/join-our-team or through Indeed.

Include a cover letter describing why you want to work for the Bill of Rights Institute. Must include your salary expectations to be considered.

If you have any questions, please contact Joanne McKinney at jrmckinney@mybri.org or Michael Torres at mtorres@mybri.org.

Our staff is passionate about what we do and for whom we do it. We constantly strive to produce the highest quality products for our teachers, students, and the community.

The Bill of Rights Institute has a unique organizational culture we value and support. Our culture is affirming and collegial. We are achievers. We enjoy working with each other; our interactions are positive, respectful, and filled with good humor. We reward excellence and achievement. We embrace all diversity in our work and our workforce. It really is that special here!
